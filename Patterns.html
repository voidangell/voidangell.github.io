Soldotna Crop

by Caitlyn Hunter

The Soldotna Crop is a top-down circular yoke sweater that features stranded colorwork. I designed this sweater with Portland’s unpredictable spring weather in mind. I look forward to layering this little top over linen dresses as well high-waisted skirts and pants. The design can easily be lengthened if crops aren’t your style, instructions are included in the pattern. This design is simple enough for an adventurous beginner, yet still engaging for advanced knitters. Best of all, the cropped length and sleeveless style make this a super quick and gratifying project. The body length is easily adjustable, keep in mind changes will alter the yardage required.

Alaska Cowl

by Susan Willsrud

This is a cozy, warm cowl—designed for Alaskan winters, but good for anywhere! It is really fast and fun to knit with three colorful Latvian braids creating a unique and beautiful look. Perfect for winter holiday gifts! This pattern was designed for bulky hand spun yarn, however any bulky yarn will work! Choose two contrasting colors. One solid and one variegated yarn works especially well. You can also use multiple strands to create a bulky weight.

Cols Capuches Alaska

by La Droguerie

Knitted hood collar in a blend of Anjou down (30% angora rabbit and 70% wool) and Plumette (70% super fine mohair, 30% polyamide) or Voilette for a glittery effect. Knitted jersey pattern with 1/1 rib trims. The hooded collar is knitted in the round except for the shortened rows which are knitted back and forth.

Marled Alaska Cowl

by Sun Daughter

The Marled Alaska Cowl is a simple knit that’s perfect for beginners or a quick project. This cowl is worked in the round using decreases, a broken ribbed pattern, and holding both ends of your skein together to create a delightfully squishy, marled material.

Alaska

by Melissa Metzbower

The enchanting dance of the Northern Lights in Alaska’s darkened skies is a breathtaking symphony of vibrant hues, a celestial masterpiece that leaves one awe-inspired by nature’s luminescent artistry. This sock pattern is both worked flat and in the round.
